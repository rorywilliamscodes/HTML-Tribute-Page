Nicholas Winton

Nicholas Winton was a British humanitarian who organized the rescue of 669 children, most of them Jewish, from Czechoslovakia during the Holocaust in 1939.

His efforts went unrecognized for decades until his wife found a scrapbook in their attic containing records of the children he had saved.

Winton's story serves as a testament to the power of one person's compassion and determination to make a difference in the world.
